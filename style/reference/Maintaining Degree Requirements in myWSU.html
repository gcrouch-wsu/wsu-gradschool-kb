Start here: What you need to know

This page is for graduate program coordinators and department staff who maintain degree requirements in myWSU. Students do not use this page directly; the requirements you enter here feed student-facing processes such as the future Program of Study submission workflow.

A complete degree requirement setup in myWSU has two pieces:

  1. Credit requirements. These are the minimum credit totals for the degree. They must meet or exceed Graduate School minimums for the degree type.
    • Master's degree university requirements
    • Doctoral degree university requirements
  2. Course sequences. These are the required course groups, elective lists, research requirements, and other course rules you configure for the degree. They feed the future student Program of Study workflow.

Most programs need both pieces. If your program only needs minimum credit totals, complete the credit requirements section, save, and stop there.

Important: Use effective terms correctly

Do not overwrite an existing active requirement row when program requirements change.

Instead, create a new Effective Term row with the + button. This keeps the old requirements available for students who started under the prior catalog while allowing new students to follow the updated requirements.

  • Each effective-term row applies to students who start in that term.
  • Students are held to the requirements tied to their start term.
  • Multiple rows create the historical record of requirement changes.

Add a new effective-term row when you change curriculum, credit totals, required courses, electives, or selection rules.

Before you begin

Navigation path: Main Menu > WSU > Advising > Minimum Degree Requirements > Degree Requirements

Gather this information before you open the page:

  • All of the program codes you will be updating. Programs use the format P1234 with no space (for example, P8045). The first letter indicates campus: E, G, P, S, T, or V.
  • Each active Program Plan under that program must have its own requirements. Program plans use the format P1234_1234 (for example, P8045_8045).
  • Total credits required for the Program Plan.
  • Minimum graded credits required for the Program Plan.
  • Minimum 500-level credits required, including graded and satisfactory/fail (S/F) credits.
  • Minimum required research credits, such as 700, 701, 702, or 800 credits.
  • Required and elective courses for each Program Plan.
  • Important: Gather Course IDs before you open myWSU. Course IDs are internal myWSU numbers (for example, 100776 for CHEM 520). Entering Course IDs directly is fast; searching the full course catalog without IDs is slow and painful. Look up IDs in OBIEE: Dashboards > WSU Graduate Professional Education > Minimum Degree Requirements, then open the OBIEE Course ID Look Up page. Bookmark this page if you update requirements often.

Part 1: Credit requirements

Complete this first. Credit requirements make the degree program appear and function in myWSU.

Navigation: Main Menu > WSU > Advising > Minimum Degree Requirements > Degree Requirements

Part 1, Step 1: Find the Program Plan

  1. Set Sub-Status to Open.
  2. Enter the Program code in the Academic Plan field and click Search to return all plans under that program.
  3. Select the specific Program Plan you need to update.
  4. Repeat this process for each active Program Plan.

Part 1, Step 2: Add the credit requirement row

  1. Click the + button in the upper right corner of the Degree Requirements area to add a requirement row.
  2. Set Effective Term to the term when these requirements begin. Use the current term if the requirements apply to new students starting now. Use a future term if the requirements start later.
  3. Enter the required credit values: Minimum Graded Credits, Minimum 500-Level Credits, Minimum Research Credits, Minimum S/F Credits, and Total Minimum Credits.
  4. Click Save.

Credit fields

FieldWhat to enter
Minimum Graded CreditsTotal A-F letter-graded course credits required for the degree. Graduate programs may allow up to six graded upper-division undergraduate credits for a thesis master's degree and up to nine for a non-thesis master's or doctoral degree.
Minimum 500-Level CreditsTotal graduate-level credits required.
Minimum Research CreditsResearch, thesis, or dissertation credits required by the program.
Minimum S/F CreditsTotal satisfactory/fail (S/F) credits required, if the graduate program requires any.
Total Minimum CreditsAt least 60 for Ph.D. and Ed.D. programs. At least 30 for master's programs.

Stop here if the program only needs basic credit requirements. After you save, the program can appear in myWSU. Continue only if you also need course sequences or special course rules.

Part 2: Course sequences

Complete this after Part 1 when the program has required courses, elective options, research courses, seminars, or distribution rules.

Course sequences provide the curriculum map for graduate degrees in myWSU. They define which courses satisfy the degree and feed student-facing pages coordinators do not edit directly, including the future Program of Study submission workflow.

Think of a sequence as one course group. For example, Sequence 1 could be required core courses, Sequence 2 could be elective options, and Sequence 3 could be a seminar or research requirement.

Part 2, Step 1: Create the first sequence

  1. Scroll to the Course Requirements section.
  2. Click the + button to add a new sequence.
  3. Enter the sequence number. Use 1 for the first group, 2 for the next, and continue in order for each distinct requirement group.
  4. Choose one completion type:
    • Use Complete 1.00 courses from the following list when the requirement is based on a number of courses.
    • Use Complete 3.00 credits from the following list when the requirement is based on credits.

Part 2, Step 2: Add courses to the sequence

Method 1: Enter the Course ID directly (faster)

  1. Type the Course ID in the Course ID field. Use OBIEE Course ID Look Up (Dashboards > WSU Graduate Professional Education > Minimum Degree Requirements) to find IDs before you start.
  2. If all students must complete one specific course, the sequence is complete after you enter that course.
  3. If students can choose from several courses, click the + button in the Courses area and add each course option to the same sequence.

Method 2: Search for the course (slower)

Use this method only when you do not have Course IDs. The search runs against the full course catalog and takes much longer than entering IDs directly.

  1. Click the magnifying glass next to Course ID.
  2. Search by Subject Area, such as CHEM.
  3. Enter the Catalog Nbr, such as 590, if you know it.
  4. Click Look Up in the search window.
  5. Select the course from the search results.
  6. Repeat for each course in the sequence.

Part 2, Step 3: Add more sequences if needed

Create a separate sequence for each distinct group of requirements:

  • Required course groups.
  • Elective option lists.
  • Seminar or research requirements.
  • Distribution areas or breadth categories.

Part 2, Step 4: Add special rules when needed

Use the Course Requirements text box when the sequence rows alone do not explain the rule clearly.

  • Students must choose courses from different sequence groups.
  • The program has distribution requirements across sequences.
  • You need to explain substitution or selection policies.

Example: "Additionally, one course from each of three sequences 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5."

Submit your work

How to submit

  1. Review the credit requirements, course sequences, and any Course Requirements text.
  2. Click Update Complete, Submit to Grad School. The status becomes Submitted.
  3. The Graduate School reviews the submission. When accepted, the status becomes Approved.
  4. ITS reviews approved submissions. ITS either Activates the requirements (they go live in myWSU) or Returns the submission for correction.

If you find a mistake after submitting:

  1. Click Changes Needed, Withdraw from Grad School. The status becomes Withdrawn.
  2. Correct the credit requirements, course sequences, or text box.
  3. Click Save.
  4. Submit again with Update Complete, Submit to Grad School.

If ITS returns your submission: Review the return reason, make corrections, save, and submit again.

Course Requirements text box

"Additionally, one course from each of three sequences 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5."

What this means for coordinators: This program requires students to complete at least three courses total, with each course coming from a different sequence. Students choose which three of the five sequences to satisfy. When students submit their Program of Study in myWSU (future workflow), these rules will guide their course selections.

Need help?

Common problems

"I can't find my program plan."

  • Confirm Sub-Status is set to Open.
  • Check that you are using the exact program code (P1234) or program plan code (P1234_1234).

"My requirements are more complex than the examples."

  • Use the Course Requirements text box for rules that cannot be represented by sequence rows alone.
  • Contact the Graduate School for guidance before submitting.

"I need to make changes after submitting."

  • See If you find a mistake after submitting in the Submit your work section above.
  • If ITS Returned your submission, review the return reason, correct the requirements, save, and submit again.

"My submission is Approved but requirements are not live yet."

  • Approved means the Graduate School signed off. Requirements go live only after ITS Activates them.
  • Wait for ITS processing. Contact the Graduate School or submit a Help with myWSU ticket if the status does not change.
